# TextCoach — Keyboard Setup Guide

The TextCoach keyboard lets you get message suggestions without leaving your messaging app. Paste conversation context, tap Suggest, and insert a response directly into the text field.

---

## iOS Setup

### Step 1: Open Keyboard Settings
Go to **Settings > General > Keyboard > Keyboards**.

### Step 2: Add the Keyboard
Tap **"Add New Keyboard..."** and select **TextCoach** from the list of third-party keyboards.

### Step 3: Enable Full Access
Tap **TextCoach** in your keyboard list, then toggle on **"Allow Full Access"**.

A confirmation dialog will appear. Tap **"Allow"** to confirm.

### Step 4: Switch to TextCoach Keyboard
In any messaging app, tap the globe icon on the bottom-left of your keyboard to cycle through keyboards until TextCoach appears. You can also long-press the globe icon and select TextCoach from the list.

### Why is Full Access Required?

TextCoach needs Full Access **solely for internet connectivity** — it connects to our servers to analyze conversations and generate suggestions. Without internet access, the keyboard cannot function.

**What Full Access does NOT do:**
- It does NOT log your keystrokes
- It does NOT monitor what you type
- It does NOT access your clipboard without your action
- It does NOT send any data until you explicitly tap "Suggest"

For full details, see our [Privacy Policy](PRIVACY-POLICY.md).

### Troubleshooting (iOS)

**Keyboard not appearing in the list:**
Make sure the TextCoach app is installed and has been opened at least once. Restart your device if needed.

**Keyboard disappears after a while:**
iOS may remove third-party keyboards from memory. Switch back to TextCoach using the globe icon. If it repeatedly disappears, go to Settings > General > Keyboard > Keyboards, remove TextCoach, and re-add it.

**"Allow Full Access" toggle not visible:**
Make sure you are tapping on TextCoach within the Keyboards list (Settings > General > Keyboard > Keyboards > TextCoach), not just the "Add New Keyboard" screen.

## Android Setup

### Step 1: Open Input Settings
Go to **Settings > System > Languages & input > On-screen keyboard**.

(On some devices this may be under **Settings > General Management > Keyboard list and default**.)

### Step 2: Enable the Keyboard
Tap **"Manage on-screen keyboards"** (or **"Keyboard list"**) and toggle on **TextCoach**.

### Step 3: Confirm Trust
A dialog will appear explaining that TextCoach may collect text you type. Tap **"OK"** to confirm.

**Note:** This is a standard Android warning for all third-party keyboards. TextCoach does NOT collect or log your keystrokes. It only processes text when you explicitly tap "Suggest". See our [Privacy Policy](PRIVACY-POLICY.md) for details.

### Step 4: Switch to TextCoach Keyboard
When you tap any text field, a keyboard icon appears in the navigation bar (or notification shade). Tap it and select **TextCoach** from the list of available keyboards.

Alternatively, you can set TextCoach as your default keyboard in **Settings > System > Languages & input > Default keyboard**.

### Troubleshooting (Android)

**Keyboard not appearing in the list:**
Make sure the TextCoach app is installed from the Play Store. Restart your device if the keyboard does not appear after installation.

**Keyboard not switching:**
Look for the keyboard icon in the bottom navigation bar or in the notification shade. If neither appears, go to Settings > System > Languages & input > Default keyboard and select TextCoach.

**Suggestions not loading:**
Ensure you have an active internet connection. TextCoach requires internet access to generate suggestions.

## How to Use the Keyboard

1. Open any messaging app (iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, etc.).
2. Switch to the TextCoach keyboard.
3. **Paste the conversation context** into the input area at the top of the keyboard.
4. Tap **"Suggest"**.
5. Review the generated suggestions, each with a different tone.
6. Tap a suggestion to **insert it directly** into the message text field.
7. Edit if needed, then send.
